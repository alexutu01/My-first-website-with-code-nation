1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects.

Any conscious process has a certain degree of uncertainty that cannot be eliminated. Uncertainty in the modern conception is a broader notion that includes both negative and positive aspects, so uncertainty cannot be confused with risk. While the risk is considered to have a negative aspect, the favorable circumstances represent the positive aspects of a process. Projects must be managed taking into account the fact that there are always favorable risks and circumstances. All the people involved in a project should be encouraged to anticipate, identify both risks and favorable circumstances and report them to the project organization. That is why the risk and opportunity management plan once developed must be applied effectively throughout the project life cycle.

Some of the risks of a project can be:

  • Technical aspects
    • the basic idea of the project is flawed
  • Financial aspects
    • unrealistic estimation of the budget needed to carry out the project
  • Managerial Aspects
    • incorrect estimation of activities, incompetence of the project manager
  • Work team formation
    • defective teamwork, low qualification of team members or failure to cover project-specific areas of competence
  • Defective technology transfer
    • non-compliance with the conditions imposed on the application of the developed technology, the obtained products do not have the forecasted characteristics

Other impediments and risks to a failed project could be that some or all of the tools listed in question 1.1 are not working properly, such as:

  • the laptop or any other device you use is not functioning correctly
  • the internet connection is unstabile
  • antivirus not instaled
  • did not backup your work

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

Data is often a company’s most critical asset. Businesses globally are collecting increasing amounts of data on customers and partners so they can be more competitive in the marketplace. In the European Union (EU), over 90% of citizens say they want some data protection rights across the EU, regardless of where their data is processed. But why? Because similar to the large data breaches that have occurred in the US , an increasing number of cases have been reported in the EU as well. Consumers around the world are concerned about how their personal data is managed.

This is where GDPR comes into place, to put simply, the goal of GDPR is to protect the rights of people giving you their data. GDPR provides an opportunity for companies to enhance their existing systems. Technology can power much of the internal change needed to help companies be more responsive and GDPR compliant. It’s important to understand that companies can never outsource their liability to a cloud service provider. Even if you hire a GDPR compliant cloud source provider, your company is still liable.

The first step is to conduct a readiness assessment of your current data protection compliance programs and set up a roadmap to determine next steps. Conducting a readiness assessment is the best way to ensure the right protective measures are taken. It should be more than just a checklist. Assessments provide a good foundation to assess the potential and ongoing risk of systems and data flows within them. Fines for breach of GDPR are substantial – up to 4% of annual global turnover.
